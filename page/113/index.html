‘Cormac McCarthy’ (born Charles McCarthy on July 20, 1933) is an American author, dramatist, and screenwriter who lives in New York City with his family. He has authored eleven books, which cover a wide range of genres, including Southern Gothic, Western, and post-apocalyptic fiction. For his novel The Road, he was awarded the Pulitzer Prize as well as the James Tait Black Memorial Prize for Fiction (2006).
