    The queen of the junk-yard Mary Padian has an innate eye for treasure. An eccentric and free-spirited treasure hunter, Mary is ready to dive-in in anything if she sees the potential for treasure. From dumpster diving to hunting for treasures in storage, Mary is always diving headstrong in search of treasure. While her process is chaotic, to say the least, Mary always gets the results in her favor. She first rose to fame following her appearances in the reality TV series, Storage Wars. She starred in a spin-off series Storage Wars Texas, before joining the main cast of Storage Wars. In this article, we will take a closer look at the wiki-bio of Mary Padian.

    Wiki/Bio Facts

    Early Life

    Mary Padian was born on 24th August 1980 in Texas, United States. Born to John and Teresa Padian, Mary shares a mixed descent. Her father, John, comes from an Irish family, while her mother, Teresa, is Lebanese. As a young kid, Mary had a normal life and enjoyed her time in Dallas. Her father operated a junkyard which set the precedence for Mary. After graduating from a local high school, Mary enrolled at the University of Texas at Austin and graduated with a degree in photojournalism in 2003.

    Professional Career

    Mary Padian is an American reality television star and a businesswoman who is best known for her appearance in the popular show Storage Wars. She is also the owner of an online store called Mary’s Finds, where she sells antique and refurbished furniture.

    Early Career: From Journalism to Design

    Straight out of the University, Mary worked as a freelance writer for D Magazine in Dallas Texas. In 2004, she moved to New York City and joined Architectural Digest, a magazine that showcases the work of top architects and designers. She was an assistant editor for the magazine for two years, working under the legendary editor-in-chief Paige Rense. She wrote and edited stories for the magazine and its website, as well as helped with the production of special issues. She also got her own section called ‘Mary Finds’ in 2007 where she posted videos on home refurbishing and decoration. During this time, Mary learned to tap into her artistic side. As she got to rummage through junk appraising and selling vintage items, Mary’s skill as a treasure hunter only continued to grow.

    After 5 years at Architectural Digest, Mary returned to Texas in 2010 and decided to set up a shop. The decision came on the back of her parent’s divorce in 2009. Back in Texas, Mary set up a shop and was mentored by Moe Prigoff.

    Storage Wars: Texas: A Breakthrough in Reality TV

    After setting up shop in 2010, Mary decided to stay in the business for two years and would cash in if she didn’t make money. Mary also closed the shop after failing to realize her goals. However, two days after closing, a woman approached Mary and asked if the stuff was hers. Mary replied that it was and the woman left the shop only to come up the next day. She revealed that she was the producer for Storage Wars: Texas and wanted Mary to film a how-to for the website. Talking about the whole experience, Mary revealed,

    “I just thought that was it, but it turned out that was my interview. I guess it’s because she knew that if she had asked me to be on a reality show, I would have been like, ‘Yeah, right.’ Then I was on the Storage Wars: Texas show. I kept my store open then, and then the Texas show ended, and they asked me to come to California. I said, ‘Why not?’ and so I’m out here now. That’s kind of it in a nutshell.”

    Following their initial conversation, Mary was cast in the reality TV show Storage Wars: Texas. Storage Wars: Texas was a spin-off of the original Storage Wars series, which featured professional buyers bidding on abandoned storage lockers in hopes of finding valuable items. Mary joined the show as a guest star in the first season in 2011. She was paired with her friend Moe Prigoff, a veteran buyer and owner of a furniture store. She quickly became a fan favorite for her quirky personality and enthusiasm. She was nicknamed “The Junkster” for her ability to find treasures in junk. She became a main cast member in the second season and appeared in 58 episodes until the show ended in 2014.

    Storage Wars: A New Adventure in California

    After the cancellation of Storage Wars: Texas, Mary moved to California to join the original Storage Wars series in 2014. She was introduced as a friend of Brandi Passante, one of the main buyers on the show. She continued to showcase her skills and charm as she competed with other buyers for storage lockers across California. She also developed friendships and rivalries with other cast members, such as Darrell Sheets, Jarrod Schulz, Ivy Calvin, and Rene Nezhoda.

    Mary is one of the most popular and successful buyers on the show. She also earned a reputation for being frugal and savvy. She often buys lockers for low prices and sells the items for high profits on her website or at flea markets. Mary appeared in over 59 episodes of Storage Wars until 2019. Since leaving the show, Mary closed the retail shop and took her business online. Mary is also associated with a charity called Ubuntu Life. The charity sells fair-trade products made by Kenyan women to raise money for a clinic targeted toward kids with neurological issues in the country.

    Mary’s Finds: A Thriving Online Business

    Besides being a reality TV star, Mary is also a successful businesswoman. She runs her online store, Mary’s Finds, where she sells antique and refurbished furniture, vintage clothing, jewelry, art, and other unique items. She also offers custom design services for her clients. She sources her products from various places, such as storage auctions, estate sales, thrift stores, and online platforms.

    Mary’s Finds has a loyal customer base and a strong online presence. Her website features a blog where she shares tips and tricks on finding and transforming furniture.  Her social media accounts have thousands of followers who admire her style and personality.
